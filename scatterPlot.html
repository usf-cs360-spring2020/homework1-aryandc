Air Traffic Demographics of passenger count from 2011-2019 using scatter plot

Data

The data is collected from DataSF and contains information on monthly passenger traffic statistics by airline at San Francisco International Airport.

"This data is made available under the Public Domain Dedication and License v1.0 whose full text can be found at: www.opendatacommons.org/licenses/pddl/1.0/"

Information about some of the data elements are given below:

  1. Activity Period: This column contains the date a record is collected. Its of the form "YYYYMM" and ranges from July, 2005 (200507) to September, 2019 (201909).
  2. GEO Summary: Contains information about the type of flight leaving SF International Airport: "Domestic" or "International".
  3. GEO Region: Provides information about where the flight is going. (USA, Europe, Canada, Mexico, Middle East, Asia,...).
  4. Passenger Count: This contains data about the passenger boarding a plane from a particular airline company. The data ranges from 1 to 660K.

Wrangling

The data is filtered by activity period as "greater than or equal to September, 2011". The data is grouped by activity period, GEO Summary, and the passenger count is aggregated by sum. 

            Filtered by:    Activity Period > September, 2011
            Grouped by:     Activity Period
                            GEO Summary
            Aggregated by:  Passenger count as sum

Instructions

The above scatter plot shows the passenger count boarding to flights from the San Francisco International Airport over a period of 9 years. The data is from September, 2011 to September, 2019. Instead of showing aggregate passenger count for a year, they are plotted for every month. The passenger count per month is also separated based on the type of flight they are boarding, i.e. "Domestic" or "International".

Here the blue circles represents the passenger count for international flights, and the red circles represents the passengers count for domestic flights, per month.

The farther the circle, from x-axis (Year) the more number of passengers boarded that flight type.

Discussion

From the scatter plot, it can be suggested that:

  1. Both domestic flights and international flights the data is seasonal in nature.
  2. The plot also show a trend where the passenger count is low at the begininng and end of every year, and higher during the middle months of year.
  3. The rate of increase in passenger count for domestic flights is more than that of international flights.
